Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions. Don't see your question here? Feel free to reach out directly.

Rabbi Asaf personally reads every message and typically responds within 48 hours. During Jewish holidays or when traveling for speaking engagements, responses may take up to 72 hours. Rest assured that every message receives a thoughtful, personal reply.

Absolutely! Rabbi Asaf welcomes Torah questions and spiritual guidance inquiries. While he cannot provide formal halachic rulings (for which you should consult your local Orthodox rabbi), he's happy to share insights, perspectives, and point you toward appropriate resources for deeper study.

We love receiving topic suggestions! Simply send an email with "Episode Suggestion" in the subject line, or use the contact form above and select "Episode topic suggestion" as your inquiry type. Rabbi Asaf carefully considers all suggestions and many listener ideas have become popular episodes.

Due to the high volume of requests, Rabbi Asaf primarily responds via email. However, for speaking inquiries, collaboration proposals, or significant educational projects, virtual meetings can be arranged. Please specify in your initial message if you're requesting a call and provide details about the purpose and expected duration.

Yes, please! Rabbi Asaf deeply values hearing how the podcast, book, or speaking events have affected people's lives. These stories are incredibly meaningful and help shape future content. You can share your story via the contact form or email, and let us know if you're comfortable with your story being shared anonymously to inspire others.

For technical issues, please email with "Technical Issue" in the subject line and include details about the problem, what device/browser you're using, and any error messages. Common issues include website loading problems, audio playback difficulties, or trouble accessing bonus content. We'll resolve technical issues as quickly as possible.

Rabbi Asaf does offer limited one-on-one learning sessions for serious students. Due to high demand, these opportunities are selective and typically reserved for individuals with specific learning goals or unique circumstances. Please reach out with details about your learning background and objectives for consideration.

Rabbi Asaf responds to messages in both English and Hebrew. While the podcast and website content are primarily in English, he's comfortable corresponding in Hebrew for those who prefer it. Please feel free to write in whichever language you're more comfortable with.
